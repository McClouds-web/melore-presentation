M

Brand Name Philosophy

MELORÉ

Pronunciation: Meh-loh-RAY

Derived from the Greek/Latin word mel (honey), which is a subtle nod to the founder’s name, Melisa. Honey has been a powerhouse beauty and skin-healing ingredient for centuries - the brand story writes itself naturally.

Why It Works

  • Sounds premium and international.
  • Easy to pronounce across Shona, Ndebele, and English.
  • High brand-extension potential (makeup, fragrance, hair).
  • Instagram handle and clean domain available.

Reserve Options

Alternative names held in reserve for future product lines or secondary branding:

Mellūra Soft & feminine
Aurmel Gold + Honey (abstract)
Velōra High-end luxury

Brand Narrative & Values

"Every woman carries gold within her. Meloré was born from the belief that beauty is not something you apply - it is something you reveal. Like honey, pure and golden, drawn from nature and refined with care, Meloré exists to help every Zimbabwean woman uncover her most luminous self."

Recommended Tagline: Your Glow, Your Gold

Our Mission

To create effective, clean cosmetics that celebrate and enhance the natural beauty of African women - products made with intention, not imitation. Formulating specifically for regional skin needs rather than adapting standard Western chemicals.

Our Vision

To become Zimbabwe's most beloved homegrown cosmetic brand, and a recognized African beauty brand across the SADC region by 2029. Setting the bar for quality, sustainability, and authentic African representations of luxury.

Authenticity

Real local ingredients, verified results, and an open founder narrative.

Inclusivity

Formulated from scratch for African skin tones, skin sensitivities, and climate conditions.

Quality

Premium packaging, refined textures, and reliable performance at an accessible price point.

Community

Co-created with and for Zimbabwean women through direct feedback loops.

Warm, Confident, & Sophisticated

Meloré is proudly African and does not feel the need to mimic Western concepts. It empowers women without being preachy or clinical. Imagine the trusted, stylish friend who always looks glowing and is happy to share her beauty secrets with you.

Warmly Inviting Unapologetically Radiant Locally Rooted

Zimbabwe Cosmetic Market

Market Summary

  • Market Size: Estimated at USD 120M – 160M annually.
  • Growth Velocity: Robust 8% – 12% annual growth, driven by rapid urbanization and rising youth/middle-class beauty expenditure.
  • Key Driver: High demand for skin-evening and natural melanin-flattering formulations. SPF awareness is growing rapidly among urban shoppers.
  • The Gap: Under-served mid-market tier (USD 10–20). Local buyers are currently stuck with expensive US/EU imports or cheap, unbranded counterfeits.

Regional Beauty Import Share

Zimbabwe depends on imports, showing a strong market gap for local premium brands.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Authentic local founder connection
  • Honey & native oils storytelling
  • Lower overhead than global importers
  • Agile, direct-to-consumer delivery

Weaknesses

  • Zero initial brand recognition
  • Limited startup marketing budgets
  • Outsourced contract formulation
  • No formal retail footprint on day one

Opportunities

  • "Buy Zimbabwean" pride trend
  • Booming beauty micro-influencer economy
  • Premium SADC expansion (Zambia, Botswana)
  • Refillable & eco-luxe product positioning

Threats

  • Established players launching budget lines
  • Raw materials supply chain import bottlenecks
  • Market flooding of counterfeit products
  • Macroeconomic currency rate shifts (USD/ZiG)

Distribution Strategy Roadmap

Priority 1: WhatsApp Commerce (Day 1)

Build WhatsApp Business catalog. Accept EcoCash, ZIPIT, and USD cash. Delivery via Vaya Africa or DHL nationwide.

Priority 2: Digital-First Social Commerce (Day 1)

Instagram and TikTok shop redirects to direct orders. Simple messaging funnel to capture organic traffic.

Priority 3: E-Commerce Storefront (Month 1-2)

Launch optimized Shopify store. Set up local payment gateway integrations (Paynow) to support cards and mobile money.

Priority 4: Salon Partnerships (Month 2-4)

Consignment displays in 5 high-foot-traffic salons in Harare (Borrowdale, Avondale, Avenues). Staff education programs.

Influencer & UGC Plan

Target 15-20 local beauty vloggers. Provide free product gifting packages in exchange for organic story shares.

Tier 2: Micro-Influencers (10K - 50K followers)

Partner with 3-5 lifestyle creators. Compensation: USD 50-150 per dedicated product demonstration reel.

Launch Campaign: "Born from Gold"

PHASE 1

Pre-Launch (Weeks 1–4)

Establish social grids. Secure @melore.beauty handle. Post mysterious golden honey drops graphics. Pre-gift product packages to nano-influencers with embargo requests until Launch Day.

PHASE 2

Launch Day (Day 1)

Coordinated posting by all gifted influencers. Live product demo on Instagram. Shopify site opens to public. Limited offer: free lip gloss for the first 50 customers to generate immediate momentum.

PHASE 3

Post-Launch (Weeks 5–12)

Weekly customer reviews and before-and-after video compilations. Launch salon pop-up stores in Harare's affluent shopping centres. Tease Phase 2 serum product lineup.
